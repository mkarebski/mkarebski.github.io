Strong technical communication reduces coordination cost.

A good status update should explain what changed, what is blocked, and what decision is needed next. It should be possible to scan it in a few seconds and still understand the risk. That matters even more during incidents, migrations, and cross-team work where ambiguity tends to compound quickly.

Clarity is usually less about writing more and more about removing everything that does not help the next person act.
